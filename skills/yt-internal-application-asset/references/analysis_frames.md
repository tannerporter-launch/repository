# Internal application asset, analysis frames

This is the analytical backbone for Worker 2. It defines the sections of the
Internal Application asset and the lenses used in the Step 5 link teardown.
Every section must be insightful, action-oriented, and free of filler.

## Section A, content DO and DO NOT

- Built from the substance of what was said in the transcript.
- Two flat lists: what to DO, and what NOT to do, each tied to a concrete moment or claim in the video.
- Each bullet is an instruction Tanner could hand to a team member, not a vague takeaway.

## Section B, form DO and DO NOT

- Built from the medium, style, structure, pacing, and the how of delivery, not the content.
- Covers hook, retention tactics, editing rhythm, on-screen text, story order, tone, calls to action, and packaging.
- Two flat lists: what to DO, and what NOT to do, about the form.

## Section C, procedural templates

- Each template is a reusable how-to Tanner can follow, written as numbered steps.
- yt-dlp note does not apply here, these are creative procedures.
- Template, How to make a video like this.
- Template, How to structure a script to recreate this type of video.
- Template, How to apply this video's information ordering and formula to other videos.
- Template, How to apply this video's sequence to sales calls.
- Template, How to apply this video's sequence to marketing sequences.
- Template, How to apply this video's sequence to other contexts.

## Section D, link and resource teardown

- One subsection per link, built from the teardown.py output, the Jina text, and the screenshot.
- Analyze each link through all nine lenses below.
- End every link with a one-line verdict.

## The nine teardown lenses

- Visual design elements, the colors, type, imagery, and polish.
- Visual structure, the layout and how the eye moves down the page.
- Psychological structure, the cognitive and emotional triggers in play.
- Informational structure, what is told, in what order, and what is withheld.
- Marketing structure, the offer framing, positioning, and proof.
- Sales structure, the path to a purchase or commitment and the friction on it.
- Persuasive structure, the specific persuasion tactics, scarcity, authority, social proof, reciprocity.
- Marketing copy and phrasing, the actual words, headlines, and microcopy worth stealing or avoiding.
- Relationship to the video, expectation fulfillment, branding consistency, red flags, and green flags between the video promise and the link experience.

## Verdict format

- End each link with: "Verdict, implement in my business" or "Verdict, do not implement" or "Verdict, adapt", plus one line of why.
- If a link is unreachable by every tier, write "Verdict, unreachable" and continue. Never fail the run over one dead link.

## Self-supplementing rule

- Write the page so it can grow. Each new asset adds to the knowledge base rather than replacing it.
- Use stable headings so a future agent can append to a section by name.
